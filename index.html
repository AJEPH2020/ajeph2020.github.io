Projects

DS Coin | Java

August 2021-November 2021

Created the DS Coin cryptocurrency, which is a nearly finished Blockchain network that supports distributed consensus and proof-of-work.
In this, a person can either be a Seller (who sells an item and receives payment), a Buyer (who purchases this item and sends payment), or a Miner (some third person who verifies and approves the transactions).
This project was completed as a component of the COL106: Data Structures and Algorithms course under the supervision of Prof. Amitabha Bagchi and Prof. Venkata Koppula.

ARM Architecture | VHDL

January 2022-April 2022

In this project, a multi-cycle ARMv4T(32bit) architecture that can execute a portion of the Data Processing, Data Transfer, Branch, Multiplication, and Interrupt instructions was designed, simulated, and synthesised in VHDL.
This was made as a part of COL216 - Computer Architecture course under Prof. Anshul Kumar.

A compiler for the WHILE language | SML, ML-Lex, ML-Yacc

January 2022-April 2022

Utilizing SML, ML-Lex, and ML-Yacc, a lexer, parser, and interpreter were created for the fictitious programming language "WHILE" (a language supporting all basic functionalities like, variable declaration, conditional statements, loops and arithmetic, logical and input-output operations).
This was done as the course project for COL226 - Programming Languages course under Prof. S. Arun Kumar.

Maze RunAround | C++, SDL, Sockets

March 2022-April 2022

A multiplayer online game based on the IITD campus was created using the SDL2 library and Sockets in C++ as a component of COP290 - Design Practices course under Prof. Riju Rekha Sen.
In this two-player game, the winner is determined by who has earned the most points at the end of the game by completing various random tasks. "Oh, is it really that easy?..." Nope :) The players also need to eat after a certain amount of time to recover their health, slow down a little to regain stamina, and score boosters in the form of mystery gift boxes, in addition to the various tasks and subtasks within...!

Audio Processing DNN | C++

January 2022-February 2022

Implemented a deep neural network in C++ to identify the top three words that each of the 12 audio keywords—silence, unknown, yes, no, up, down, left, right, on, off, stop, and go—classifies to from the individual one-second audio clips (as [1x250] input matrix).
This was done as a part of COP290 - Design Practices course under Prof. Riju Rekha Sen.

Signal Modulation and Demodulation | Matlab

January 2022-April 2022

This project implements all three modulation and demodulation algorithms (i.e., amplitude-shift keying (ASK), which represents digital data as variations in the amplitude of a carrier wave, frequency shift keying (FSK), in which the carrier signal's frequency changes in response to changes in the digital signal, and phase shift keying (PSK), in which the carrier signal's phase is changed by varying the sine and cosine inputs at a specific time) in Matlab as a part of ELL205 - Signals and Systems course under Prof. Abhishek Dixit.
